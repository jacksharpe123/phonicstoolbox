Teacher Guidance ℹ️

RWI Phonics Framework

Pure Sounds: Ensure sounds are pronounced cleanly without an 'uh' at the end (e.g. 'mmmm' not 'muh', 's' not 'suh'). This drastically reduces cognitive load when pupils are blending words.

Stretchy vs. Bouncy: Set 1 sounds are introduced as either continuous/stretchy (m, s, n, f, l, r, v, z, sh, th, ng) or short/bouncy (a, e, i, o, u, b, c, d, g, h, j, p, q, t, w, x, y, ch).

Special Friends: Emphasize "Special Friends" (two or three letters making one sound) before pupils read a word. The visual 'chips' in the practice sections map these clearly.

Fred Talk: Guide pupils to sound out words using pure sounds before blending (e.g. c-a-t = cat). For older pupils, internalize this to "Fred in your head".

Missing Sound Builder 🧩

This tool develops spelling accuracy by focusing on grapheme position. For example, the /ai/ sound is typically spelled 'ay' at the end of a word (play) but 'ai' in the middle (snail). Encouraging older pupils to recognize these orthographic patterns builds independence and directly supports Entry Level writing targets.

Look, Cover, Write, Check 🙈

This spelling tool builds orthographic memory. Words are presented in chunks of 5, scaling in difficulty. The word flashes for 3 seconds, is covered, and the pupil must recreate it using the keyboard. Errors are shown to the pupil so they can re-map the visual pattern.
